Know Your Blooms

Understanding flower meanings and occasion-appropriate selections

The Language of Flowers

Before we dive into arrangement techniques, it's important to understand the symbolic meanings behind different flowers and how to select the right blooms for specific occasions. This knowledge will elevate your floral designs from merely beautiful to deeply meaningful and perfectly appropriate.

Flowers have been used to communicate feelings and messages for centuries. This tradition, known as "floriography" or the language of flowers, became especially popular during the Victorian era when direct expressions of emotion were often constrained by social etiquette.

Popular Blooms and Their Meanings

Most Popular
Roses

Love, respect, courage (varies by color)

Perfect for:
  • Romantic occasions
  • Anniversaries
  • Weddings
Lilies

Purity, rebirth, elegance

Perfect for:
  • Sympathy arrangements
  • Easter celebrations
  • Weddings
Tulips

Perfect love, new beginnings

Perfect for:
  • Spring celebrations
  • Birthdays
  • New home gifts
Sunflowers

Adoration, loyalty, longevity

Perfect for:
  • Summer celebrations
  • Congratulations
  • Friendship gestures
Daisies

Innocence, purity, loyal love

Perfect for:
  • New baby arrangements
  • Children's celebrations
  • Cheerful get-well bouquets
Elegant
Orchids

Luxury, beauty, strength

Perfect for:
  • Sophisticated gifts
  • Housewarmings
  • Business arrangements

Flowers for Special Occasions

Weddings

Traditional wedding flowers symbolize love, purity, and new beginnings:

Roses Peonies Lilies Stephanotis Hydrangeas

Sympathy

Flowers for sympathy arrangements convey respect, remembrance, and comfort:

White Lilies Chrysanthemums Carnations Gladioli White Roses

Birthdays

Birthday flowers should be vibrant and celebratory, reflecting the recipient's personality:

Gerbera Daisies Sunflowers Tulips Carnations Birthday Month Flower

Anniversaries

Different wedding anniversaries have traditional flower associations:

1st: Carnations 5th: Daisies 10th: Daffodils 25th: Iris 50th: Yellow Roses

Get Well

These flowers bring cheer and hope during recovery:

Daisies Daffodils Peonies Gerberas Sunflowers

Congratulations

Celebratory arrangements to mark achievements and successes:

Stargazer Lilies Yellow Roses Sunflowers Iris Alstroemeria
